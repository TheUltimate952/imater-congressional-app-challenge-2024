Time: 18-20 minutes

City: Jacksonville

Ingredients
  • 4 redfish fillets (about 6 ounces each)
  • 2 tablespoons Cajun seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges (for serving)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish, optional)
    •

Cajun Blackened Redfish

Preparation
  1. Season the fish: Pat the redfish fillets dry with paper towels. Rub Cajun seasoning evenly over both sides of each fillet.
  2. Heat the skillet: In a large cast-iron skillet, heat the butter and olive oil over medium-high heat until the butter is melted and the skillet is hot.
  3. Cook the fish: Carefully add the seasoned redfish fillets to the skillet. Cook for about 3-4 minutes on each side, or until the fish is cooked through and has a blackened crust. The internal temperature should reach 145°F (63°C).
  4. Serve: Remove the fish from the skillet and transfer to a serving platter. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side.
