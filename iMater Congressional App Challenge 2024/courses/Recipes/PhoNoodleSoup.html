Appetizers Entrees Desserts

Time: 2-3 hours

City: Orlando

Ingredients
  • 1 pound beef bones (or chicken bones)
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 1 ginger root, halved and charred
  • 4-6 cups water
  • 2-3 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • Salt to taste
  • 8 ounces rice noodles (bánh phở)
  • 1 pound thinly sliced beef (e.g., sirloin or brisket)
  • Fresh herbs (basil, cilantro, mint)
  • Bean sprouts
  • Sliced jalapeños or Thai chili peppers
  • Lime wedges
  • Sriracha and hoisin sauce (for serving)
    •

Pho Noodle Soup

Preparation
  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, add beef bones (or chicken bones), halved onion, and charred ginger. Cover with water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for about 1-2 hours, skimming off any foam that rises to the top.
  2. Add spices: Add star anise, cinnamon stick, fish sauce, and salt to the broth. Continue to simmer for another 30 minutes to 1 hour, allowing the flavors to meld.
  3. Cook the noodles: In a separate pot, cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. Strain the broth: Once the broth is done, strain it through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the solids. Return the clear broth to the pot and keep warm.
  5. Assemble the bowls: In serving bowls, add a portion of the cooked rice noodles and top with the thinly sliced raw beef. Pour the hot broth over the noodles and beef, allowing the heat to cook the beef.
  6. Garnish and serve: Top the pho with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, sliced jalapeños, and lime wedges. Serve with Sriracha and hoisin sauce on the side for extra flavor.
