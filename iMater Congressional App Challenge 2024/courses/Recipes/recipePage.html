Time: 40 minutes

Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients
  • 2 cups of pre-cooked cornmeal (Harina PAN or Masarepa)
  • 2 cups of warm water
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoons of butter (softened)
  • Optional: cheese (grated), for filling or topping
  • Optional: vegetable oil or more butter for frying

Colombian Arepas

Preparation
  1. Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the pre-cooked cornmeal and salt.
  2. Add water: Slowly pour the warm water into the cornmeal mixture while stirring with a spoon or your hands. Make sure no lumps form.
  3. Knead the dough: Once the dough is thick, knead it by hand for about 2-3 minutes. The dough should be smooth, soft, and not too sticky. If the dough is too dry, add a little more water, and if it's too wet, sprinkle a little more cornmeal.
  4. Add butter: Knead in the softened butter to make the dough even softer and tastier.
  5. Shape the arepas: Divide the dough into equal portions, about the size of a golf ball or larger, depending on your preference. Flatten each portion into a disc, about 3/4 inch thick and 4-5 inches in diameter.
  6. Cook the arepas:
      Stovetop: Heat a non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Add a little vegetable oil or butter to the pan. Cook each arepa for about 5-7 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside.
      Oven option: You can also bake the arepas at 350F (175C) for 15-20 minutes after cooking them on the stove, to ensure they're fully cooked inside.
  7. Serve: Arepas can be served plain, or you can slice them open and stuff them with cheese, meats, or avocado. They also taste great with a little butter or fresh cheese on top.
