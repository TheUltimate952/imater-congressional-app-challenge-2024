Appetizers Entrees Desserts

Time: 40 minutes

City: St. Augustine

Ingredients
  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cucumber, grated and excess moisture squeezed out
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried dill)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
    •

Tzatziki

Preparation
  1. Prepare the cucumber: Grate the cucumber using a box grater. Place the grated cucumber in a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth and squeeze out excess moisture to prevent the tzatziki from becoming watery.
  2. Mix the ingredients: In a mixing bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, minced garlic, olive oil, dill, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  3. Chill: Cover and refrigerate the tzatziki for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  4. Serve: Serve chilled as a dip with pita bread, vegetables, or as a sauce for gyros and other dishes.
